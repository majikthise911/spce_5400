## Apology and Methodology

**I made a mistake.** I scored CRYPTO-SAT as 5/5 novelty ("first space-based quantum RNG") but then admitted:
- Ground-based quantum RNGs already exist and work great
- China's Micius satellite already did quantum key distribution from space (2016)
- Space doesn't actually add significant value for RNG

**That's not 5/5 novelty. That's inflated scoring.**

### New Novelty Evaluation Methodology

For each mission, I will ask:
1. **Does this technology already exist?** (ground or space)
2. **Has a similar mission already flown?** (any country, any organization)
3. **What's actually novel?** (be specific and honest)
4. **Is the novelty significant or incremental?**

**Scoring (honest version)**:
- **5/5**: Completely unprecedented - nothing like this exists anywhere
- **4/5**: Highly novel - similar concepts exist but significant new approach/application
- **3/5**: Moderate innovation - builds on existing tech with meaningful improvement
- **2/5**: Incremental improvement - derivative of existing missions/tech
- **1/5**: Not novel - already exists, just replicating
