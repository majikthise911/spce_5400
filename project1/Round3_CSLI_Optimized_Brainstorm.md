# Round 3: CSLI-Optimized Mission Brainstorming
**Date:** 2025-11-02
**Focus:** Missions specifically designed to excel on CSLI requirements while maintaining innovation

---

## Brainstorming Strategy

Based on CSLI rubric analysis, optimal missions should:

✅ **Strong NASA Alignment** - Earth Science, Technology Development, or enabling future missions
✅ **Simple** - Minimize complex systems (avoid propulsion, pyrotechnics, multiple novel systems)
✅ **LEO Flexible** - Any low Earth orbit works (sun-sync preferred but not required)
✅ **COTS Heavy** - Use off-the-shelf components with flight heritage
✅ **Affordable** - Target <$100k total (ideally <$75k)
✅ **Standard Ground Station** - Amateur radio bands (437 MHz, 140 MHz)
✅ **Standard Licensing** - FCC + NOAA (if imaging)
✅ **2U-3U Preferred** - Sweet spot for cost, complexity, capability

**Your Interests to Incorporate:**
- Teslas/autonomous vehicles
- Adventure/exploration
- Space and multiplanetary civilization
- Space mining enablers
- DoD/national defense applications
- Nature and environmental protection
- Practical problem-solving

## NEW MISSION CONCEPTS

### **Mission 21: "AURORA SHIELD" - Radiation Storm Early Warning (3U)**

**Problem**: Solar storms damage satellites, power grids, and endanger astronauts; current warning systems are ground-based with limited coverage

**Solution**: Constellation of CubeSats with energetic particle detectors creating 3D map of radiation environment in real-time; provides earlier warning than ground stations

**Innovation**: First distributed LEO radiation monitoring network; AI predicts storm intensity and trajectory

**NASA Alignment**:
- Space Weather (top CSLI science priority)
- Protects NASA assets (ISS, satellites)
- Heliophysics research

**Design Parameters**:
- **Form Factor**: 3U
- **Payload**: Energetic particle detector (COTS available from radiation research)
- **Orbit**: Any LEO (higher altitude = better coverage)
- **Ground Station**: Standard amateur radio (437 MHz)
- **Cost Estimate**: ~$60k (particle detector + COTS subsystems)

**CSLI Optimization**:
- ✅ Simple payload (single instrument)
- ✅ COTS particle detectors with flight heritage
- ✅ Extremely flexible orbit requirements
- ✅ Clear NASA benefit (Space Weather priority)
- ✅ Standard ground station
- ✅ Straightforward licensing

**Unique Angle**: Protects both NASA assets AND Starlink constellation (commercial partnership potential)

### **Mission 22: "TERRA-BYTE" - High-Speed Disaster Data Relay (2U)**

**Problem**: During natural disasters (hurricanes, earthquakes, wildfires), ground communications fail; first responders need connectivity

**Solution**: LEO relay providing emergency communications via store-and-forward; prioritizes disaster data from ground sensors and first responders

**Innovation**: AI-powered message prioritization (medical emergencies first); integrates with existing amateur radio emergency networks

**NASA Alignment**:
- Earth Science - disaster monitoring and response
- Communications technology development
- Societal benefit (disaster response)

**Design Parameters**:
- **Form Factor**: 2U
- **Payload**: Software-defined radio (COTS) + data storage + AI processor
- **Orbit**: LEO sun-synchronous preferred but not required
- **Ground Station**: Amateur radio (compatible with emergency networks)
- **Cost Estimate**: ~$45k

**CSLI Optimization**:
- ✅ Very simple - mostly software innovation
- ✅ All COTS components (SDR, storage, processor)
- ✅ Flexible orbit
- ✅ Amateur radio heritage
- ✅ Clear societal benefit
- ✅ Low cost

**Unique Angle**: Builds on Tesla's Starlink disaster response but fills coverage gaps; amateur radio community collaboration
